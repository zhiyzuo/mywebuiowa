Projects

Research Collaboration in Multidisciplinary Institutions

06/2015 - Present

Interdisciplinarity has been a very popular concept, especially for research institutions and funding agencies. While there are many many ways to measure the level of interdisciplinarity, there are just too many variables to control. In this study, we are especially interested in the interdisciplinarity of collaborations in multidisciplinary environments. Again, we use U.S. iSchools, which are perfect case study here due to its multidisciplinary nature. We first inspect the level of collaborations within each iSchool. Next we propose two types of multidsiciplinarities: educational and research. We then correlate the two metrics with the level of colalboration. Finally, we measure collaboration interdisciplinarity in a network context and correlate this with multidisciplinarity.

Disruption Propagation in Supply Chain Network

10/2015 - 01/2016

It is very important for managers to understand the propagation of disruptions in their firms' supply networks to minimize possible losses. Our research aims at 1) understanding the flow of disruptions; 2) proposing a network-topology-based metric of disruption propagation; 3) introducing proactive re-wiring strategies for individual firms. The data we used is from Mergent Online. Concretely, 2971 firms are collected, along with their supply-demand and competition relationship. An agent-based model is designed and multiple simulations were run to answer our research questions.

The Current Status of U.S. Information Schools

11/2014 - 7/2015

As a PhD student in Information Science, it is interesting and important for me to understand the evolution of information schools (a.k.a iSchools). What is information science sociey doing? This is a very difficult question -- people in iSchools have diverse backgrounds: library science, computer science, management, biostatistics, engineering, to name a few. Therefore, we would like to see what is the current compostiion of iSchool faculty members from two perspetives: training backgrounds (PhD programs) and research topics. We also investigate the revolution of their research topics over a 27-year period, starting from 1988 (the time when Gang of Three formed) to 2014. Finally, we examine whether iSchools tend to hire doctorate graduates from similar iSchools.
